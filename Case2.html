Case Study #2

Customer Order General Analysis

For case study #2, we are analysing the customer orders csv page that includes four columns: index, customer email, net revenue, year. Through analysing the data objects, we can see that the index is not really useful as it does not include any relevant information except for keeping track of the rows in the table. Instead, we can consider the customer email (disregarding situations in which users have multiple or changed emails) as a unique identifcation of a user. The net revenue is given as a float and we can use this information to see whether the user was  a customer for the specific year. In regards to year, we see that it has only 3 unique values, 2015, 2016, 2017 meaning that the data supplied is for the time period from 2015 to 2017. 

I have supplied some supplemental information such as the data types of the database and the top 5 rows in the database to show context. 2017 Net Revenue Data

Customer Turnover

From looking at the data above for 2015-2017, we can see that the customer turnover is pretty big and each year loses quite a bit of customers with the customer churn being above 70% each year

Revenue Change

Despite the high customer turnover, the existing customer growth has been positive meaning that the existing customers are spending more money, while the majority of the total revenue comes from new customers

New Customers

Each year the company has been getting a significant number of new customers and despite the company losing more customers than gaining in 2016, the company bounced back with a net positive increase in customers.

Customer Change

As we can see in the image to the left, in 2016 the company lost more existing customers than it gained and because of that the company had a slight decrease in number of customers, while in 2017 the company bounced back with around 229,000 new customers beating the 184,000 lost customers they had that year. Based on the trends we can see in the graph, if the company continues to gain more customers and works to increase its customer retention, the company's financials would become significantly stronger.  

Customer Revenue

As can be seen from the box plot to the left, the statistics for net revenue for each year seems to be pretty consistent and their underlying range and variance seem to be close to identical as can be seen in the graphs. This figure shows us that most consumers spend around the same each year and there has been no change of net revenue spending as time has passed. The company can definitely work on this by trying to push consumers to spend more as this will significantly affect the total revenue of the company.
